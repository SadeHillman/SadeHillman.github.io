Foundations Reflections

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

1. Ive learnt I have a lot of growing to do with my EQ. Learning that EQ involves things like managing emotions and having self-confidence has helped me to see where I can improve by having a bit of faith in myself and answers.
2. I've learned the value of recognizing my strengths and weaknesses. Writing them down has shown me that I often underestimate myself and don't give myself enough credit.
3. I also learned that it's super important to celebrate my progress. Before, I would be really tough on myself and only see my flaws, making me feel like I wasn't good enough. But during this course, I've tried to stay positive and recognize how much I've improved.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self awareness in learning and programming?

As a student, I've discovered that understanding who I am, my values, and being self-aware is really important in figuring out how I learn and interact with others, especially in programming where collaboration is key. Empathy plays a vital role in programming as well. It's about connecting with my teammates on a deeper level. When I can understand their perspectives and put myself in their shoes, it strengthens our relationship. We can relate to each other better and offer support when needed. It's all about creating a positive and supportive environment that encourages teamwork.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

I was really surprised by two things. First, I used to think EQ was just about being good at socializing and understanding others (which I think I'm pretty good at), but it turned out to be much more than that. It made me realize that I need to work on positive self-talk and building up my confidence. The second thing that caught me off guard was the importance of listening and looping. I realized that when someone is talking to me, I really have to concentrate and make sure I remember everything they said. It got me thinking about how I'm always rushing around, trying to do a million things at once and multitasking. I need to slow down, be more present, and focus on one thing at a time.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

It was really easy spend lots of time looking up information because I really like reading about psychology and how you can improve yourself. I sometimes find typing out what I’ve found into a blog hard as well because I’m worried that it doesn’t make sense. I think this was another challenge for me and it relates to my self-confidence (i.e being worried about how will people perceive what I’ve written).

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

As much as I hated talking about myself, I don't feel like the time I spent studying core learning here was a waste. In fact, it was one of the reasons I chose to join Dev Academy. I believe that these skills are super important because they can be applied to anything in life, not just programming. Having core learning mixed into each sprint is actually a nice change of pace. If we only did coding for five weeks straight, it could get overwhelming. So, having that variety is beneficial. Plus, it gives us a chance to reflect on what we've learned, which is a really important part of the learning process. Overall, I think core learning is valuable. It helps us become more well-rounded learners and gives us time to think about what we've learned. It's definitely not a waste of time!
